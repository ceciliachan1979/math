\subsection*{Exercise 3 (Cecilia)}
$ \gcd((x + yi), (x - yi)) = \gcd((x + yi), 2x) = 1 $ since $ \gcd(x, y) = 1 $.

By Exercise 2, $ (x + yi)(x - yi) = z^2 => x + yi = e \alpha^2 $

\begin{eqnarray*}
  x + yi &=& e \alpha^2   \\
         &=& e (u + vi)^2 \\
         &=& e (u^2 + 2uvi -v^2)
\end{eqnarray*}

Units of Gaussian integers are $ 1, -1, i, -i, $ so changing $ e $ only permute the solution.
