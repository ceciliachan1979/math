\subsection*{Dummit Exercise 1.3.13 (Cecilia)}

($\Rightarrow$) Suppose $g$ has order 2. Decompose $g$ into a product of disjoint cycles $c_i$. Then:

\[
  e = g^2 = \prod c_i^2
\]

Since the cycles are disjoint, $c_i^2 = e$ for all $i$. Therefore each $c_i$ is a 2-cycle. Disjoint cycles commute, so $g$ is a product of commuting 2-cycles.

($\Leftarrow$) Suppose $g$ is a product of commuting 2-cycles $c_i$. Then:

\[
  g^2 = \prod c_i^2 = \prod e = e
\]

Therefore $g$ has order 2. \qed
